## 📋 Document Purpose

This document explores **sustainable business models** for Open-Omniscience that:
1. ✅ **Generate revenue** for development and maintenance
2. ✅ **Support news organizations** (drive traffic, provide tools, create new revenue streams)
3. ✅ **Avoid cannibalizing** newspaper subscriptions or ad revenue
4. ✅ **Align with open-source principles** (GPLv3 compliance)
5. ✅ **Uphold ethical journalism** standards (Munich Charter, ETHICS.md)

**These are IDEAS ONLY.** Implementation would require:
- Community discussion and consensus
- Legal review (especially for GPLv3 compliance)
- Ethical review against Munich Charter and ETHICS.md
- Technical feasibility assessment

## 🎯 Core Principles for Any Business Model

### ✅ Must Do
- **Support investigative journalism** as the primary mission
- **Respect all ethical guidelines** in ETHICS.md
- **Comply with GPLv3 license** requirements
- **Protect news organizations'** existing revenue streams
- **Maintain transparency** in all operations
- **Preserve user privacy** (no tracking, local processing)

### ❌ Must NOT Do
- **Compete with news orgs** for subscriptions or ad revenue
- **Syndicate full articles** that replace original consumption
- **Violate copyright** or terms of service of scraped sites
- **Collect or sell user data**
- **Create closed-source derivatives** that violate GPLv3
- **Accept unethical advertising** or sponsorships

#### Idea 1: Enterprise Support & Maintenance Contracts

**Concept:** Offer paid support contracts to organizations using Open-Omniscience in production.

**Implementation:**
```
Tier Structure:
- Basic: $5,000/year - Email support, 24-hour response, bug fixes
- Professional: $20,000/year - Priority email, 4-hour response, feature requests
- Enterprise: $50,000+/year - 24/7 phone support, 1-hour response, dedicated account manager
```

**Services:**
- Technical support (installation, configuration, troubleshooting)
- Bug fixes and security patches
- Feature requests (prioritized for paying customers)
- Training and onboarding
- Performance optimization
- Compliance auditing

**Revenue Protection:**
✅ Provides tools to enhance journalism, doesn't replace content  
✅ Helps news orgs be more efficient and effective  
✅ No content syndication - only software support

**Feasibility:** High - Proven model for open-source projects

**Potential Revenue:** $500K - $2M annually

#### Idea 10: Ethical Advertising & Sponsored Content

**Concept:** Offer ethical, non-intrusive advertising opportunities to organizations that align with Open-Omniscience's mission.

**⚠️ HIGH RISK:** This model has the **highest risk** of compromising ethical principles. It should only be considered if other models prove insufficient, and must be implemented with **extreme caution**.

**Acceptable Advertising:**
- Sponsored Tools: Tools and services that complement Open-Omniscience (ethical hosting, privacy-focused VPNs)
- Job Board: Job listings for journalism, research, and ethics-focused roles
- Event Sponsorships: Sponsorship of Open-Omniscience events and webinars
- Newsletter Sponsorships: Ethical sponsors for project newsletters

**Unacceptable Advertising:**
- ❌ News organizations (competes with their own ad revenue)
- ❌ Political organizations or campaigns
- ❌ Companies with poor ethical records
- ❌ Surveillance or privacy-violating services
- ❌ Any content that could compromise journalistic integrity

**Advertising Guidelines:**
1. **Mission Alignment:** All advertisers must align with Open-Omniscience's ethical principles
2. **Transparency:** Clearly label all sponsored content
3. **Non-Intrusive:** Ads must not interfere with user experience
4. **No Tracking:** No user tracking or data collection for advertising
5. **Editorial Independence:** Advertisers have no influence on project content or direction

**Pricing:**
- Sponsored Tool Listing: $500 - $5,000/month
- Job Board Listing: $200 - $2,000/month
- Event Sponsorship: $1,000 - $20,000/event
- Newsletter Sponsorship: $500 - $5,000/issue

**Revenue Protection:**
✅ Does not compete with news org ads  
⚠️ Neutral impact on news org revenue  
✅ Only accepts mission-aligned sponsors

**Feasibility:** Medium - Requires careful vetting

**Potential Revenue:** $100K - $1M annually

**Next Steps:**
- [ ] Develop strict advertiser vetting process
- [ ] Create public advertiser guidelines
- [ ] Establish community oversight mechanism
- [ ] Design non-intrusive ad formats
- [ ] Implement transparency reporting
