## 0. The honest frame

Two truths the audit keeps in view at once: (1) this app's discipline —
method+caveat+n on every signal, no composite scores, provenance everywhere,
fail-closed ethics — is genuinely rare and is the right spine for a
truth-seeking instrument; (2) honesty labels do not by themselves make
outputs true, representative, or understood. The audit therefore asks not
"does the app lie?" (it does not, by construction) but "where can a careful
user still be honestly misled — by sampling, by tooling limits, by their own
loop — and what do we owe them next?"

**Verdict:** the strongest pattern in the app is *stated method + stated
limit*; the two ❌ are disclosure gaps, not method flaws — cheap, high-duty
fixes (B1).

## 2. Can the app be tampered with?

- **At rest:** SQLCipher default; threat model honestly scoped (protects a
  seized/off machine, never a compromised live session). Sound.
- **Evidence:** custody chains and oo-backup-2 manifests are tamper-EVIDENT
  (hashes, signatures, Merkle); imports verified-not-trusted. Sound.
- **The live DB is writable by any process running as the user.** Known,
  stated, and unfixable at app level — but DETECTION is buildable: the
  article-hash substrate already exists, so a **local fixity audit** (re-hash
  the corpus, compare, report — the mirror's discipline turned inward) is a
  cheap, honest win (B2). Caches/analytics tables are derived and rebuildable
  — out of scope for fixity, said so.
- **Source-side tampering (the overlooked vector):** a hostile host can
  cloak — serve our honest UA different content than browsers see. We store
  what we received (hash at capture proves what WE saw, not what others
  saw). Mitigations, all consented and queued as design notes: occasional
  cross-transport spot-checks; the future mirror's witness model;
  never silent — a detected divergence is a FINDING (B6).
- **Supply chain:** no CDNs (everything local) ✅; pip deps audited weekly in
  CI ✅; bundled data (gazetteer, calendars, El Niño) carries provenance ✅.

## 3. Performance after long use

T1 fixed the reported scale (6.4k articles / 228k mentions). Honest unknowns
at 10–20× (a year of continuous collection):
- `keyword_mentions` ~3–4M rows: reads are covering-indexed; the diagnostics
  export is streamed; UNMEASURED: briefing recompute, corpus windows, FTS
  rebuild times at that scale.
- New growth surfaces SINCE T1: per-revision wiki full text (by ruling;
  compressed; grows with edit activity — disclosed in-UI), weather cache
  (bounded slices), dumps (user-chosen, sizes shown).
- No retention policy exists — **by design** (never silently delete
  evidence); but a year-scale corpus needs a CONSENTED archiving design
  (cold-store partitions, user-driven), not ad-hoc deletion.
**Action (B3):** extend `scripts/perf_harness.py` with a 100k-article
profile, measure the named paths, fix what fails the existing thresholds,
and DESIGN (not build) consented archiving. Measure first — the T1 lesson.

## 4. What data is missing? (sources that would extend the world view)

Ranked by truth-seeking value ÷ integration cost, all open/ethical:
1. **Official statistics** (designed: SDMX/APIs, vintages, comparability
   guards) — the single biggest worldview extension; treat-as-controversial
   stance already recorded.
2. **Scholarly layer:** OpenAlex/Crossref/arXiv (open APIs) for preprints +
   papers, **plus retraction data (Retraction Watch/Crossref)** — a
   truth-seeking app should KNOW when cited science was withdrawn.
3. **Fact-check feeds (ClaimReview/IFCN)** — ingested AS SOURCES WITH A
   STANCE, never as oracles (their verdict is their claim, triangulable
   like any other).
4. **Press-release wires** (PRNewswire-class, official org newsrooms) — not
   for "news" but as ORIGIN DETECTORS: lineage gains the ability to say
   "this story is a reprinted press release" (provenance gold).
5. **Parliamentary/court records** (extends the law vertical to
   proceedings; official APIs where they exist).
6. **NGO/IGO reporting** (ReliefWeb-class aggregators, open).
7. **Recorded as deliberately OUT for now:** social media (ToS hostility,
   manipulation density, robots-incompatible collection — a reasoned
   omission to state in docs, not a blind spot); video/audio (the app is
   TEXT-ONLY today and must say so — §6.8); satellite imagery (heavy;
   sister-project scale).

## 5. Neutrality & representation — can we measure it?

**The honest position: "neutral" is not definable absolutely.** Every
baseline (population share, internet-user share, press-output share) is
itself a normative choice. So the app should never claim neutrality — it
should MEASURE REPRESENTATION AGAINST DECLARED, PLURAL BASELINES and show
the user where they stand:
- per-country/language corpus share vs ≥2 stated baselines, side by side;
- concentration (Gini/entropy — the `signals.concentration` engine exists);
- **single-origin share** (what fraction of apparent corroboration collapses
  to one origin — the links/lineage substrate makes this computable TODAY);
- wire-dependence share; temporal regularity of collection;
- per-language ANALYTICAL DEPTH (which tools actually function for each
  language — the honesty matrix from §6.3, surfaced to the user).
Descriptive forever, corrective never: auto-reweighting would be silent
editorializing. This becomes the **"Your lens" dashboard** (plan A3 + B4).

## 6. The aggregator's own biases — named, with corrections

1. **Catalog selection bias** — measured (coverage report), de-US-centring
   ongoing. *Correct by:* coverage metric as acceptance gate (already
   ruled), Wikidata gap run.
2. **Permissive-host survivorship bias** — fail-closed robots ethics means
   the corpus over-represents hosts that ALLOW crawling; much paywalled/
   restrictive journalism is structurally absent. *Cannot be "fixed"
   ethically; must be DISCLOSED* (one sentence on coverage surfaces) and
   mitigated via official APIs/archives (B1+§4).
3. **Language-tooling bias** — stoplists ×16 but **CJK segmentation is
   absent: zh/ja keyword extraction is effectively nonfunctional** while
   the UI ships in those languages. *Correct by:* optional segmenters
   (jieba-class) as an `[analysis]`-style extra + per-language capability
   disclosure (B5; the matrix in §5).
4. **Recency bias** — history starts at install. *Mitigate:* dumps (done),
   archives later, the mirror long-term; disclose on trends ("your record
   begins YYYY-MM").
5. **Lexical bias** — bag-of-words; negation/quotes uncounted (plan A6).
6. **Popularity bias** — volume ≠ importance; the undertold bucket is the
   designed counterweight (keep; measure its usage).
7. **User-selection loop** — the C2 contradiction; answered by A3, never by
   auto-curation.
8. **Modality bias** — text-only; images get EXIF honesty only. *Disclose
   app-wide (B1); media verification is a future vertical.*
9. **Geocoding bias** — gazetteer favors large cities; country stand-ins
   are precision-labeled already. *Keep measuring; extend gazetteer.*
10. **Wikipedia systemic bias** — entering the corpus (by ruling) imports
    its known skews; per-edition sources keep it filterable; cite the
    documented-bias literature in the manual (B1).

**Correctable via updates?** 1,3,4,5,9,10: yes, concretely (named fixes).
2,6,7,8: structural — the correction IS disclosure + user agency + the
plural-baseline dashboard; pretending an update "fixes" them would itself
be dishonest.
