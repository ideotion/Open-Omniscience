## 1. Intended purpose

**Open Omniscience** ("the Software") is designed as an **ethical** tool serving:

- **investigative journalism** and **public-interest research**;
- **transparency** and **accountability** towards **powerful actors** and **public
  bodies**;
- the analysis of **published sources** (media, institutions, markets, law) to reveal
  their measurable **structure** — concentration, coordination/echo, novelty, framing,
  change over time — **for a human to interpret**.

The Software **surfaces measurements accompanied by their method and caveats**; it
**renders no verdict**. It is "a microscope, not a detector."

## 2. Strictly prohibited uses

The User **shall not**, and shall not enable a third party to, use the Software for the
following purposes (non-exhaustive list):

1. **Surveillance of natural persons**, tracking or following individuals.
2. **Harassment**, intimidation, or **disclosure of personal data** ("*doxxing*").
3. **Stalking** or any form of cyber-violence.
4. **Profiling of private persons** (as opposed to the analysis of public figures
   acting in the public sphere, and only on published sources).
5. **Biometric identification** (facial, voice, etc. recognition).
6. **Unlawful interception** of communications or ingestion of **private channels**
   (private messages, non-public feeds, non-public data).
7. **Circumvention** of `robots.txt` files, the terms of use of sites, or any
   technical protection measure.
8. Any infringement of **third-party rights**: copyright, neighbouring rights, the
   *sui generis* database right, privacy, image rights, trade secrets, defamation.
9. Any violation of the **law on personal-data protection**.
10. Any **unlawful, fraudulent or malicious** use, or any use contrary to **public
    policy**.

> These red lines overlap with those held **by construction** in the project (see
> [`../../GOVERNANCE.md`](../../GOVERNANCE.md): no tracking of individuals, no
> biometrics, no ingestion of private channels, no automated verdict). No technical
> safeguard is, however, infallible: **compliance with this Charter is the User's
> responsibility**.

## 3. Undertakings and acknowledgments of the User

By accepting this Charter, the User expressly **acknowledges and accepts** the
following:

1. **Sole responsibility.** They are **solely responsible** for their use of the
   Software and its outputs, as well as for **compliance with the law** applicable in
   their jurisdiction(s).
2. **Warning of the risks.** They have been **duly warned** of the **limits** of the
   Software and of the **risks** associated with inappropriate use, in particular the
   **probabilistic and fallible** nature of the outputs produced by AI (see **article 7
   of the [Terms of Use](CGU.md)**) and the fact that **imputing** an automated
   characterisation to an identifiable person may engage their **liability** (in
   particular for **defamation**).
3. **Verification.** They undertake to **verify independently** any output before
   relying on it, publishing it or drawing a decision from it.
4. **Indemnification.** They accept the **indemnification** clause set out in
   **article 10 of the [Terms of Use](CGU.md)**, within the limits permitted by law.

## 4. Consequence of misuse

4.1. Any use contrary to this Charter constitutes a **breach of the [Terms of
Use](CGU.md)**.

4.2. **Any separate permission** the Publisher might grant for the **use** of the
Software (as opposed to the licence of the code) **terminates automatically** in the
event of misuse.

4.3. **No effect on the GPL v3 licence.** This termination **in no way affects** the
rights the **GNU GPL v3** grants the User over the **code** (to run, study, modify,
redistribute). This Charter **adds no restriction** to the licence of the code and
does not condition it; it governs only the **conduct** of the User. In the event of a
conflict concerning the code licence, **the GPL v3 prevails**.
